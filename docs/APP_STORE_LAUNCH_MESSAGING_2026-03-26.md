# App Store Launch Messaging Workshop (2026-03-26)

This document turns the current Tend product story into launch-ready App Store messaging.

It is not meant to be the final, immutable copy set. It is the first disciplined pass:
- clear enough for first submission,
- honest enough for App Review,
- specific enough to market the app well,
- broad enough that we do not overfit the listing to one niche use case like entrepreneurship.

## 1. Core positioning

### The product promise
Tend is a Christian prayer app that helps people pair prayer with concrete action and visible growth.

This is the core idea:
- prayer should not stop at intention,
- faith should move into action,
- small daily follow-through should become visible and encouraging.

### The loop we are selling
Reflection -> Decision -> Action -> Feedback -> Repeat

In product terms, the loop is:
- reflection for meaning,
- scripture for grounding,
- prayer for alignment,
- one next step for action,
- plant growth and streaks for feedback,
- journaling for visible progress over time.

### The emotional outcome
The user should feel:
- "I am not just praying about this. I am moving."
- "My faith has direction."
- "Small consistent action is changing my life."

## 2. Messaging guardrails

### What to lean into
- Prayer paired with action
- Lived faith, not passive intention
- Daily consistency
- Scripture-grounded encouragement
- Visible progress through plant growth, streaks, and journal history

### What to avoid
- Do not over-market the app as only for entrepreneurs. Entrepreneurship is a good screenshot example, not the whole product category.
- Do not promise outcomes like answered prayer, healing, financial success, or guaranteed transformation.
- Do not make the app sound like a generic habit tracker with Christian wallpaper. The differentiation is prayer plus action plus reflection.
- Do not use confrontational copy too early in the listing. Strong hooks are useful in ads and social content, but the App Store page still has to feel welcoming and trustworthy.

## 3. Metadata strategy

## 3.1 App name

Current working name:
- `Tend: Pray. Act. Grow.`

This is strong enough for launch. It is memorable, short enough, and already explains the brand rhythm.

## 3.2 Subtitle

The subtitle should do one job: clarify category and benefit.

Recommended subtitle:
- `Christian prayer with action`

Why this is the best launch option:
- It explains the category immediately.
- It differentiates the app from passive devotional framing.
- It stays under Apple’s 30-character limit.

Strong alternates:
- `Prayer that leads to action`
- `Daily prayer with action`
- `Daily prayer for lived faith`

What I would not use for launch:
- `Daily prayer & faith goals`

Reason:
- "faith goals" is not wrong, but it sounds more abstract and productivity-flavored than the actual product.

## 3.3 Promotional text

Promotional text is optional. It sits above the description and can be changed without a new binary submission.

Recommendation:
- Use it.

Reason:
- It gives us one editable line for launch messaging, seasonal campaigns, and future pricing or feature announcements.
- It does not need to carry search weight. It needs to sharpen the story.

Recommended promotional text:
- `Personalized prayer journeys with scripture, reflection, and one concrete next step each day.`

Strong alternates:
- `Pray with direction, take one concrete step, and watch your journey grow.`
- `Turn prayer into one faithful next step each day.`
- `Build a daily rhythm of prayer, action, and visible growth.`

Launch guidance:
- Use the first option for App Store launch because it is clearest.
- Save the punchier hooks for ads, social posts, and future experiments.

## 3.4 Description

Recommended launch description:

`Tend is a Christian prayer app for people who want prayer to shape real life.

Instead of stopping at intention, Tend helps you pair prayer with one concrete step each day.

Start a personalized prayer journey around a goal, burden, decision, or area of growth. Each day, Tend gives you:

• a reflection grounded in your journey
• a scripture passage for focus
• a guided prayer
• one simple next step to follow through

As you keep tending your journey, your plant grows, your streak builds, and your journal shows the story of your progress over time.

Use Tend to stay engaged with what you are praying about in work, relationships, habits, calling, healing, and everyday faith. Set reminders, add widgets, and keep your journey visible throughout the day.

Tend helps you:
• pray with direction
• act with consistency
• see small growth over time

Prayer is not just something you say. Tend helps you live it.`

### Description notes
- The opening clearly states category and differentiation.
- The middle section proves the product with concrete feature structure.
- The ending lands on identity and lived-faith language without overclaiming.

## 3.5 Keywords

Keywords should support discovery with concrete search terms, not slogans.

Recommended keyword set:
- `devotional,scripture,reflection,bible,faith,goals,habit,discipline,journey,reminder`

Why this set works:
- It stays under Apple’s 100-byte limit.
- It covers adjacent prayer-intent searches without repeating the exact brand framing too heavily.
- It includes both spiritual and behavior-oriented terms.

Strong alternate set:
- `devotional,scripture,reflection,bible,faith,goals,habit,journey,streaks,widget`

Keyword strategy notes:
- If subtitle stays `Christian prayer with action`, avoid repeating too much of that language in keywords.
- Revisit keywords after real impressions and search term data, not before.

## 4. Screenshot strategy

## 4.1 What the screenshots must prove

The screenshot set should answer four questions fast:
1. What is this app?
2. What makes it different from other prayer apps?
3. What does daily use actually look like?
4. Why would I keep coming back?

The product page should visually prove:
- this is a prayer app,
- it gives daily guided content,
- it asks for action,
- it rewards consistency,
- it stays present through reminders and widgets.

## 4.2 Screenshot style direction

Use the existing app visuals, but keep the screenshot copy cleaner and more editorial than app UI copy.

Direction:
- One short headline per frame
- One support line only if needed
- Show real app UI, not poster art pretending to be UI
- Use the app’s plant, journal, reminder, and widget visuals as proof
- Keep the first two screenshots emotionally strong and immediately differentiating

## 4.3 Recommended screenshot narrative

If we do 6 screenshots, use this sequence.

### Screenshot 1: Position the app
Goal:
- Establish the category and the difference immediately.

Recommended overlay:
- `Prayer that moves into action`

Support line:
- `Personalized journeys with reflection, scripture, prayer, and one next step.`

Recommended screen:
- Home screen with plant, journey title, and Today card visible

### Screenshot 2: Show the daily structure
Goal:
- Prove this is not vague inspiration. It has a real daily format.

Recommended overlay:
- `Reflect. Pray. Take one next step.`

Support line:
- `Each journey gives you daily reflection, scripture, prayer, and action.`

Recommended screen:
- Journal detail showing reflection, scripture, prayer, and Tend prompt

### Screenshot 3: Show action
Goal:
- Make the "prayer plus action" idea concrete.

Recommended overlay:
- `Close the gap between prayer and follow-through`

Support line:
- `Commit to one simple step each day.`

Recommended screen:
- Today card / reveal step UI / action prompt state

### Screenshot 4: Show visible growth
Goal:
- Prove the feedback loop and emotional reward.

Recommended overlay:
- `See your consistency grow`

Support line:
- `Plants, streaks, and journal history make progress visible.`

Recommended screen:
- Plant growth plus streak component, or home plus journal list if cleaner

### Screenshot 5: Show reminders and widgets
Goal:
- Show how the app stays present during the day.

Recommended overlay:
- `Keep your journey in front of you`

Support line:
- `Use reminders and widgets to stay engaged.`

Recommended screen:
- Widget preview and reminder setup screen

### Screenshot 6: Show personalization
Goal:
- End with ownership and identity.

Recommended overlay:
- `Make each journey your own`

Support line:
- `Customize the look and grow a prayer journey that feels personal.`

Recommended screen:
- Background customization and themed plant visual

## 4.4 If we want a 7th screenshot

Use it for the journal/history angle:

Headline:
- `Track the story of your growth`

Support line:
- `Return to past entries and see what changed over time.`

Screen:
- Journal list or detail view that clearly shows dated entries

## 4.5 What not to do in the screenshots

- Do not lead with widgets. They support retention, but they are not the core differentiation.
- Do not lead with reminders. Same reason.
- Do not make screenshot one feel like a habit tracker or productivity planner.
- Do not use overly aggressive challenge copy on the first screenshot.
- Do not show UI that implies a feature we are not actually shipping yet.

## 5. App previews

Recommendation for first launch:
- Skip app previews unless we have time to make them polished.

Reason:
- Apple makes app previews optional.
- A mediocre preview hurts more than it helps.
- The launch-critical asset is a strong screenshot sequence with clear copy.

If we do create a preview later, the story should be:
1. Start a journey
2. See reflection, scripture, prayer, and step
3. Complete a Tend
4. Watch the plant grow
5. Add reminder and widget

## 6. Brand voice for the App Store

The App Store tone should be:
- grounded,
- specific,
- hopeful,
- disciplined,
- emotionally clear,
- not overly churchy,
- not combative.

Good launch phrases:
- `Prayer that moves into action`
- `One faithful next step each day`
- `See your consistency grow`
- `A daily rhythm of prayer, action, and visible growth`
- `Personalized prayer journeys for real life`

Phrases to save for ads or social testing:
- `Stop praying about things you never act on`
- `What if your prayers are waiting on your actions?`
- `I realized most of my prayers were not unanswered. I just was not moving.`

Those lines are strong, but sharper than what I would lead with on the first App Store listing.

## 8. Future tests after launch

These are good candidates once the app is live and we have real data:

### Metadata tests
- Subtitle test:
  - `Christian prayer with action`
  - vs `Prayer that leads to action`
- Promotional text test:
  - softer clarity line
  - vs stronger challenge-based line

### Screenshot tests
- General life framing vs entrepreneurship-led example journey
- Plant-growth-led screenshot first vs prayer-structure-led screenshot first
- Reminder/widget proof in one frame vs split across two frames

### Monetization-related tests
- Pricing tests
- Trial framing tests
- Paywall headline and proof hierarchy

Those should happen after launch, not before.

## 9. Immediate next asset work

To move from strategy to finished assets, the next practical work is:
1. Lock subtitle
2. Lock promotional text
3. Capture the 6 target screens in a clean state
4. Write final screenshot overlay copy in one visual system
5. Export one iPhone screenshot set at the highest required size and let Apple scale down where appropriate
6. Only make an app preview if the screenshots already look polished
